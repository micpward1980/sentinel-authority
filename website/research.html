White Paper

When Self-Certification Fails

Lessons from Boeing 737 MAX for the Age of Autonomous Systems

This white paper examines the structural certification failure that caused two Boeing 737 MAX crashes, killing 346 people. Drawing on the U.S. House Transportation Committee’s 238-page investigation and Department of Justice proceedings, it identifies the same self-certification vulnerability emerging across the autonomous systems landscape—and introduces ODDC as the independent conformance framework designed to prevent aviation’s failures from repeating in the age of autonomous systems.
