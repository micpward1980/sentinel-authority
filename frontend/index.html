The Physics of Permission.

ODDC — Operational Design Domain Conformance — certifies that autonomous systems operate only within their defined operational envelope, enforced at runtime by ENVELO (Enforced Non-bypassable Verified Envelope Limit Operation) with auditable evidence. Sentinel Authority governs the standard.

THE FRAMEWORK

ODDC + ENVELO

ODDC (Operational Design Domain Conformance) is the certification. ENVELO (Enforced Non-bypassable Verified Envelope Limit Operation) is the runtime enforcement mechanism. Together, they ensure autonomous systems operate only within defined boundaries—with proof.

ODDC Certification

  • Operational Design Domain — the formally specified boundary of permitted autonomous action.
  • Conformance verification — evidence that the system respects its ODD at runtime.
  • Auditable record — signed certification artifact for regulators, insurers, and counterparties.
  • Revocable — certification suspended upon material deviation or drift.

ENVELO Enforcement

  • Non-bypassable interlock — validates every action before execution.
  • Fail-closed behavior — out-of-domain actions are blocked or overridden.
  • Continuous monitoring — runtime state compared against ODD constraints.
  • Tamper-evident logging — all boundary interactions recorded for audit.

CERTIFICATION CRITERIA

What ODDC Certification requires

ODDC certification is granted only when the Operational Design Domain is formally defined, evidence supports stable operation within it, and ENVELO runtime enforcement is present and auditable.

Minimum criteria

ODD formally specified
Operational Design Domain defined with objective constraints and tolerances.
Evidence of stable behavior within ODD
Convergence/stability thresholds satisfied across relevant operational regimes.
ENVELO enforcement present
Non-bypassable interlock validates actions before execution; fail-closed behavior defined.
Auditability & traceability
Signed authorization record + tamper-evident audit log reference.
Revocation & drift handling
Clear rules for suspension/revocation upon ODD violation, drift, or audit failure.
Material Deviation

Any state, trajectory, or enforcement failure that exceeds certified tolerances or violates defined invariants.

CERTIFICATION PROCEDURE

Convergence Authorization Test (CAT-72)

ODDC certification is issued only after successful completion of the Convergence Authorization Test (CAT-72), a formal validation event establishing bounded autonomous behavior across operational regimes.

Validation Requirements

  • 72-hour continuous convergence validation — sustained operation within ODD bounds.
  • Multi-regime operational stress testing — performance across edge conditions and transitions.
  • Fail-closed ENVELO enforcement on material deviation — verified halt behavior outside ODD.
  • Cryptographically verifiable certification evidence — tamper-evident validation record.

Issuance & Renewal

CAT-72 completion is required for both initial ODDC certification and periodic renewal. Waivers are not issued.

GRANT INSTRUMENT

What ODDC Certification confers

ODDC certification is structured to support underwriting review as a first-class risk control for autonomous infrastructure.

Certification Rights

  • Certified Operational Design Domain — facility + domain scope definition.
  • Bounded autonomous actuation rights — permitted actions within certified ODD.
  • Signed evidence artifact — for regulators, insurers, and counterparties.
  • Defined validity term — time-bounded certification window.
  • Revocable upon material deviation or drift — continuous conformance requirement.

Certification terms are issued based on operational scope, ODD complexity, and domain risk profile.

RUNTIME ENFORCEMENT

How enforcement works

ENVELO is enforced as a non-bypassable control boundary: predict → evaluate → execute. Unsafe actions are blocked or overridden before actuation.

Phase 01
Signal interception

Model outputs are routed through the interlock before reaching actuator interfaces.

Phase 02
ODD validation

Proposed actions are evaluated against the certified ODD and trajectory limits.

Phase 03
Fail-closed enforcement

Out-of-domain actions are blocked or overridden; events are logged for auditability.
